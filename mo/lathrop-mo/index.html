Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Maintaining the exterior of your home in Lathrop Missouri is crucial, but common problems with exterior paint can affect both appearance and protection. At Painting Vikmanic, we help homeowners identify and address these issues to keep their homes looking fresh and well-protected.
Peeling and Cracking
Peeling and cracking are among the most common issues with exterior paint. They often result from poor surface preparation, moisture intrusion, or using low-quality paint. This problem not only detracts from your home’s curb appeal but also exposes the underlying surface to weather damage. Regular maintenance and choosing the right paint type can help prevent peeling and cracking.
Fading and Discoloration
Over time, exterior paint can fade or discolor due to sun exposure, harsh weather, and pollutants. Darker colors are especially prone to fading in the intense sunlight of Lathrop Missouri . To combat this, use high-quality, UV-resistant paints designed for exterior use, and consider lighter shades that hold up better under sunlight.
Blistering Paint
Blistering occurs when paint bubbles up from the surface, usually due to painting on a hot surface, trapped moisture, or using the wrong primer. Blisters can compromise the paint’s protective layer, making it vital to fix the underlying issue before repainting.
Mildew and Mold Growth
In humid areas of Lathrop Missouri mildew and mold can grow on exterior surfaces, leaving unsightly black or green stains. Regular cleaning, proper ventilation, and using mold-resistant paint can help keep these growths at bay.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking can be a frustrating issue for homeowners in Lathrop Missouri but understanding the underlying causes can help you address and prevent it effectively. At Painting Vikmanic, we’re committed to ensuring your painted surfaces maintain their beauty and durability.
Moisture Infiltration
Moisture is a leading cause of paint peeling. When water infiltrates behind the paint, it disrupts adhesion, leading to peeling or flaking. This can occur due to leaks, high humidity, or condensation. Areas exposed to rain or high moisture, such as exterior walls or bathrooms, are particularly susceptible.
Improper Surface Preparation
Surface preparation is crucial for a successful paint job. If the surface was not thoroughly cleaned and prepped, including the removal of old paint, dirt, or grease, the new paint may not adhere properly. This inadequate preparation can result in peeling as the paint fails to bond with the surface.
Low-Quality Paint
Using low-quality paint can also lead to peeling issues. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more likely to fail over time. Opting for high-quality, durable paint products can help prevent peeling and extend the life of your paint job.
Application Problems
Adverse weather conditions or improper application techniques, such as applying paint too thickly or in unsuitable temperatures, can cause peeling. Ensuring the paint is applied under the right conditions and in appropriate layers helps avoid these problems.
