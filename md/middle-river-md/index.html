Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint not only enhances the curb appeal of your home but also protects it from harsh weather conditions. At Painting Vikmanic, serving all cities in Middle River Maryland we understand that various issues can arise with exterior paint over time. Here’s a guide to some common problems and how to address them.
Peeling and Flaking
Peeling and flaking paint are often caused by inadequate surface preparation or moisture infiltration. When the underlying surface isn’t properly cleaned or primed, paint can struggle to adhere, leading to unsightly peeling. Additionally, if moisture gets trapped under the paint layer, it can cause flaking. To prevent this, ensure surfaces are thoroughly cleaned, primed, and that any underlying issues are addressed before repainting.
Fading and Discoloration
Sunlight and weather exposure can cause paint to fade and lose its vibrant color. This is particularly noticeable on the sides of your home that face direct sunlight. Choosing high-quality, UV-resistant paints can help maintain the color and protect your home from the elements in Middle River Maryland ’s varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint forms bubbles on the surface, often due to heat or moisture. This issue typically arises from painting on a wet or hot surface. To avoid blistering, ensure that the surface is completely dry and that you paint in moderate weather conditions.
Mold and Mildew Growth
In humid areas of Middle River Maryland mold and mildew can develop on exterior paint, especially in shaded or damp areas. This not only affects the aesthetic appeal but can also lead to long-term damage. Use mold-resistant paint and ensure proper ventilation and drainage to minimize these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking can be a frustrating issue for homeowners in Middle River Maryland but understanding the underlying causes can help you address and prevent it effectively. At Painting Vikmanic, we’re committed to ensuring your painted surfaces maintain their beauty and durability.
Moisture Infiltration
Moisture is a leading cause of paint peeling. When water infiltrates behind the paint, it disrupts adhesion, leading to peeling or flaking. This can occur due to leaks, high humidity, or condensation. Areas exposed to rain or high moisture, such as exterior walls or bathrooms, are particularly susceptible.
Improper Surface Preparation
Surface preparation is crucial for a successful paint job. If the surface was not thoroughly cleaned and prepped, including the removal of old paint, dirt, or grease, the new paint may not adhere properly. This inadequate preparation can result in peeling as the paint fails to bond with the surface.
Low-Quality Paint
Using low-quality paint can also lead to peeling issues. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more likely to fail over time. Opting for high-quality, durable paint products can help prevent peeling and extend the life of your paint job.
Application Problems
Adverse weather conditions or improper application techniques, such as applying paint too thickly or in unsuitable temperatures, can cause peeling. Ensuring the paint is applied under the right conditions and in appropriate layers helps avoid these problems.

Middle River, MD Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Middle River is an unincorporated community and census-designated place in Baltimore County, Maryland, United States. The population was 33,203 at the 2020 census. A Middle River Train Station first appeared on the 1877 G.M. Hopkins & Co Baltimore County Map and by 1898 had a designated Post Office and modest street grid. The town expanded during the 1930s and 1940s and established the 'Aero Acres' housing community which borders the railroad tracks and Martin Blvd. to serve as housing for people working at the Martin Aerospace Company. For this development, the architecture firm of Skidmore, Owings & Merrill designed gable-roofed Cape Cod houses with dimensions of 24 feet (7.3 m) by 28 feet (8.5 m), featuring large commercial-style windows in their principal rooms. In 1941 a total of 600 homes were built at Aero Acres using this design from Cemento (a cement/asbestos material).
