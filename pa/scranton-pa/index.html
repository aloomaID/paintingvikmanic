Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint is your home’s first line of defense against the elements, but even the best paint job can face issues over time. At Painting Vikmanic, we’ve seen it all, and we’re here to help homeowners in Scranton Pennsylvania understand and address the most common problems with exterior paint.
Peeling and Cracking
Peeling and cracking are among the most common issues, often caused by moisture, improper surface preparation, or the use of low-quality paint. When water seeps under the paint, it can cause the layers to separate from the surface, leading to peeling. Cracking typically begins as hairline fractures that widen over time, compromising your home’s exterior.
Fading and Discoloration
Exposure to the sun’s UV rays can cause paint to fade and discolor, particularly on south-facing walls. Fading not only dulls the appearance of your home but also indicates that the paint’s protective qualities are diminishing. High-quality, UV-resistant paint is crucial to maintaining vibrant color in Scranton Pennsylvania 's varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint bubbles up from the surface, often due to excessive heat, moisture, or painting over a damp surface. These blisters can burst, leading to peeling and further damage. Proper surface preparation and painting under optimal weather conditions are key to preventing this issue.
Mildew and Mold Growth
In humid areas of Scranton Pennsylvania mildew and mold can grow on exterior surfaces, particularly in shaded areas. This not only affects the appearance of your home but can also lead to structural damage if left untreated. Using mildew-resistant paint and ensuring proper drainage can help prevent these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking is a common issue that homeowners in Scranton Pennsylvania may face. At Painting Vikmanic, we understand the frustration this problem can cause and are here to help you identify and address the root causes effectively.
Moisture Infiltration
Moisture infiltration is one of the leading causes of paint peeling. When water penetrates behind the paint, it can weaken the adhesion and cause the paint to lift off. This is particularly problematic in areas with high humidity or where water leaks are present, such as bathrooms or exterior walls exposed to rain.
Improper Surface Preparation
Inadequate surface preparation is another major culprit. For paint to adhere properly, the surface must be thoroughly cleaned, dry, and free of debris or old paint. Failing to prepare the surface correctly can result in poor adhesion and lead to peeling or flaking paint over time.
Low-Quality Paint
The use of low-quality paint can significantly contribute to peeling issues. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more likely to fail. Choosing high-quality, durable paint products can reduce the risk of peeling and ensure a longer-lasting finish.
Application Problems
Painting under unsuitable conditions or applying paint too thickly can also lead to peeling. Extreme temperatures, high humidity, or overly thick layers can prevent proper curing and adhesion, resulting in paint failure.
To avoid paint peeling or flaking, focus on proper surface preparation, invest in high-quality paint, and ensure optimal application conditions.
