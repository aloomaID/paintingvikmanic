Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint not only enhances the curb appeal of your home but also protects it from harsh weather conditions. At Painting Vikmanic, serving all cities in Drexel Hill Pennsylvania we understand that various issues can arise with exterior paint over time. Here’s a guide to some common problems and how to address them.
Peeling and Flaking
Peeling and flaking paint are often caused by inadequate surface preparation or moisture infiltration. When the underlying surface isn’t properly cleaned or primed, paint can struggle to adhere, leading to unsightly peeling. Additionally, if moisture gets trapped under the paint layer, it can cause flaking. To prevent this, ensure surfaces are thoroughly cleaned, primed, and that any underlying issues are addressed before repainting.
Fading and Discoloration
Sunlight and weather exposure can cause paint to fade and lose its vibrant color. This is particularly noticeable on the sides of your home that face direct sunlight. Choosing high-quality, UV-resistant paints can help maintain the color and protect your home from the elements in Drexel Hill Pennsylvania ’s varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint forms bubbles on the surface, often due to heat or moisture. This issue typically arises from painting on a wet or hot surface. To avoid blistering, ensure that the surface is completely dry and that you paint in moderate weather conditions.
Mold and Mildew Growth
In humid areas of Drexel Hill Pennsylvania mold and mildew can develop on exterior paint, especially in shaded or damp areas. This not only affects the aesthetic appeal but can also lead to long-term damage. Use mold-resistant paint and ensure proper ventilation and drainage to minimize these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking is a common issue that homeowners in Drexel Hill Pennsylvania may face. At Painting Vikmanic, we understand the frustration this problem can cause and are here to help you identify and address the root causes effectively.
Moisture Infiltration
Moisture infiltration is one of the leading causes of paint peeling. When water penetrates behind the paint, it can weaken the adhesion and cause the paint to lift off. This is particularly problematic in areas with high humidity or where water leaks are present, such as bathrooms or exterior walls exposed to rain.
Improper Surface Preparation
Inadequate surface preparation is another major culprit. For paint to adhere properly, the surface must be thoroughly cleaned, dry, and free of debris or old paint. Failing to prepare the surface correctly can result in poor adhesion and lead to peeling or flaking paint over time.
Low-Quality Paint
The use of low-quality paint can significantly contribute to peeling issues. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more likely to fail. Choosing high-quality, durable paint products can reduce the risk of peeling and ensure a longer-lasting finish.
Application Problems
Painting under unsuitable conditions or applying paint too thickly can also lead to peeling. Extreme temperatures, high humidity, or overly thick layers can prevent proper curing and adhesion, resulting in paint failure.
To avoid paint peeling or flaking, focus on proper surface preparation, invest in high-quality paint, and ensure optimal application conditions.
