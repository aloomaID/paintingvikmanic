Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint not only enhances the curb appeal of your home but also protects it from harsh weather conditions. At Painting Vikmanic, serving all cities in Malad City Idaho we understand that various issues can arise with exterior paint over time. Here’s a guide to some common problems and how to address them.
Peeling and Flaking
Peeling and flaking paint are often caused by inadequate surface preparation or moisture infiltration. When the underlying surface isn’t properly cleaned or primed, paint can struggle to adhere, leading to unsightly peeling. Additionally, if moisture gets trapped under the paint layer, it can cause flaking. To prevent this, ensure surfaces are thoroughly cleaned, primed, and that any underlying issues are addressed before repainting.
Fading and Discoloration
Sunlight and weather exposure can cause paint to fade and lose its vibrant color. This is particularly noticeable on the sides of your home that face direct sunlight. Choosing high-quality, UV-resistant paints can help maintain the color and protect your home from the elements in Malad City Idaho ’s varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint forms bubbles on the surface, often due to heat or moisture. This issue typically arises from painting on a wet or hot surface. To avoid blistering, ensure that the surface is completely dry and that you paint in moderate weather conditions.
Mold and Mildew Growth
In humid areas of Malad City Idaho mold and mildew can develop on exterior paint, especially in shaded or damp areas. This not only affects the aesthetic appeal but can also lead to long-term damage. Use mold-resistant paint and ensure proper ventilation and drainage to minimize these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking is a common issue that homeowners in Malad City Idaho may encounter, and understanding the causes can help you prevent or address this problem effectively. At Painting Vikmanic, we’re dedicated to providing solutions for maintaining the beauty and integrity of your painted surfaces.
Moisture Infiltration
One of the primary causes of paint peeling is moisture infiltration. When water seeps behind the paint, whether through leaks, condensation, or high humidity, it can cause the paint to lose adhesion and begin to peel or flake. This is particularly common in areas prone to high moisture levels, such as bathrooms or exterior walls exposed to rain.
Improper Surface Preparation
Another major factor is inadequate surface preparation. For paint to adhere properly, the surface must be clean, dry, and free of old paint, dirt, or grease. If the surface was not properly prepared before painting, the new paint may not stick correctly, leading to peeling or flaking over time.
Low-Quality Paint
Using low-quality paint can also contribute to peeling issues. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more prone to failure. Investing in high-quality, durable paint products can significantly reduce the risk of peeling and extend the life of your paint job.
Application Issues
Painting in adverse weather conditions or applying paint too thickly can also result in peeling. Extreme temperatures, high humidity, or applying paint layers that are too thick can prevent proper curing and adhesion, causing the paint to fail.
To prevent paint peeling or flaking, ensure proper surface preparation, choose high-quality paint, and apply it under suitable conditions.
