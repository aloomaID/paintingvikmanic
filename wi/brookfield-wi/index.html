Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint not only enhances the curb appeal of your home but also protects it from harsh weather conditions. At Painting Vikmanic, serving all cities in Brookfield Wisconsin we understand that various issues can arise with exterior paint over time. Here’s a guide to some common problems and how to address them.
Peeling and Flaking
Peeling and flaking paint are often caused by inadequate surface preparation or moisture infiltration. When the underlying surface isn’t properly cleaned or primed, paint can struggle to adhere, leading to unsightly peeling. Additionally, if moisture gets trapped under the paint layer, it can cause flaking. To prevent this, ensure surfaces are thoroughly cleaned, primed, and that any underlying issues are addressed before repainting.
Fading and Discoloration
Sunlight and weather exposure can cause paint to fade and lose its vibrant color. This is particularly noticeable on the sides of your home that face direct sunlight. Choosing high-quality, UV-resistant paints can help maintain the color and protect your home from the elements in Brookfield Wisconsin ’s varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint forms bubbles on the surface, often due to heat or moisture. This issue typically arises from painting on a wet or hot surface. To avoid blistering, ensure that the surface is completely dry and that you paint in moderate weather conditions.
Mold and Mildew Growth
In humid areas of Brookfield Wisconsin mold and mildew can develop on exterior paint, especially in shaded or damp areas. This not only affects the aesthetic appeal but can also lead to long-term damage. Use mold-resistant paint and ensure proper ventilation and drainage to minimize these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking is a frustrating issue that many homeowners in Brookfield Wisconsin face. Understanding the underlying causes can help you prevent or address these problems effectively. At Painting Vikmanic, we’re here to provide expert solutions to keep your paint job looking pristine.
Moisture Infiltration
One of the leading causes of paint peeling is moisture infiltration. Water entering behind the paint, whether from leaks, condensation, or high humidity, can disrupt the paint’s adhesion, leading to peeling or flaking. This is especially problematic in areas with high moisture exposure, such as bathrooms or exterior walls subjected to rain.
Improper Surface Preparation
Inadequate surface preparation is another critical factor. For paint to adhere properly, the surface must be clean, dry, and free of old paint, dirt, or grease. Without thorough preparation, the new paint may not bond effectively, resulting in peeling or flaking over time.
Low-Quality Paint
Using low-quality paint can also contribute to peeling. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more prone to failure. Opting for high-quality, durable paint products can significantly reduce the risk of peeling and enhance the longevity of your paint job.
Application Issues
Adverse weather conditions or applying paint too thickly can cause peeling. Extreme temperatures, high humidity, or overly thick layers of paint can hinder proper curing and adhesion, leading to paint failure.
To prevent paint peeling or flaking, ensure proper surface preparation, use high-quality paint, and apply it under optimal conditions.
