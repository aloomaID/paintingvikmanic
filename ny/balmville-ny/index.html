Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint not only enhances the curb appeal of your home but also protects it from harsh weather conditions. At Painting Vikmanic, serving all cities in Balmville New York we understand that various issues can arise with exterior paint over time. Here’s a guide to some common problems and how to address them.
Peeling and Flaking
Peeling and flaking paint are often caused by inadequate surface preparation or moisture infiltration. When the underlying surface isn’t properly cleaned or primed, paint can struggle to adhere, leading to unsightly peeling. Additionally, if moisture gets trapped under the paint layer, it can cause flaking. To prevent this, ensure surfaces are thoroughly cleaned, primed, and that any underlying issues are addressed before repainting.
Fading and Discoloration
Sunlight and weather exposure can cause paint to fade and lose its vibrant color. This is particularly noticeable on the sides of your home that face direct sunlight. Choosing high-quality, UV-resistant paints can help maintain the color and protect your home from the elements in Balmville New York ’s varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint forms bubbles on the surface, often due to heat or moisture. This issue typically arises from painting on a wet or hot surface. To avoid blistering, ensure that the surface is completely dry and that you paint in moderate weather conditions.
Mold and Mildew Growth
In humid areas of Balmville New York mold and mildew can develop on exterior paint, especially in shaded or damp areas. This not only affects the aesthetic appeal but can also lead to long-term damage. Use mold-resistant paint and ensure proper ventilation and drainage to minimize these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking can be a frustrating issue for homeowners in Balmville New York but understanding the underlying causes can help you address and prevent it effectively. At Painting Vikmanic, we’re committed to ensuring your painted surfaces maintain their beauty and durability.
Moisture Infiltration
Moisture is a leading cause of paint peeling. When water infiltrates behind the paint, it disrupts adhesion, leading to peeling or flaking. This can occur due to leaks, high humidity, or condensation. Areas exposed to rain or high moisture, such as exterior walls or bathrooms, are particularly susceptible.
Improper Surface Preparation
Surface preparation is crucial for a successful paint job. If the surface was not thoroughly cleaned and prepped, including the removal of old paint, dirt, or grease, the new paint may not adhere properly. This inadequate preparation can result in peeling as the paint fails to bond with the surface.
Low-Quality Paint
Using low-quality paint can also lead to peeling issues. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more likely to fail over time. Opting for high-quality, durable paint products can help prevent peeling and extend the life of your paint job.
Application Problems
Adverse weather conditions or improper application techniques, such as applying paint too thickly or in unsuitable temperatures, can cause peeling. Ensuring the paint is applied under the right conditions and in appropriate layers helps avoid these problems.

Balmville, NY Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Balmville is a hamlet (and census-designated place) in Orange County, New York, United States. It is part of the Poughkeepsie–Newburgh–Middletown, NY Metropolitan Statistical Area as well as the larger New York–Newark–Bridgeport, NY-NJ-CT-PA Combined Statistical Area. It is located in the southeastern part of the Town of Newburgh. The population was 3,197 at the 2020 census. Many wealthy, influential, and upper income families live in Balmville on roads such as River Road, Sloane Road, Commonwealth Avenue, Susan Drive, and Grand Avenue. Susan Drive is accredited for housing the former Delano Family Estate (Algonac). The Delano family was the family of the mother of Franklin D. Roosevelt, and she in fact was born and raised at Algonac. Many homes in Balmville are incredibly expensive due to their pristine views of the Hudson River. Balmville is also the site of the Powelton Club Country Club. It currently ranks as the highest income hamlet in the greater Newburgh area.
