Common Problems With Exterior Paint

Exterior paint is your home’s first line of defense against the elements, but even the best paint job can face issues over time. At Painting Vikmanic, we’ve seen it all, and we’re here to help homeowners in Del Rio Texas understand and address the most common problems with exterior paint.
Peeling and Cracking
Peeling and cracking are among the most common issues, often caused by moisture, improper surface preparation, or the use of low-quality paint. When water seeps under the paint, it can cause the layers to separate from the surface, leading to peeling. Cracking typically begins as hairline fractures that widen over time, compromising your home’s exterior.
Fading and Discoloration
Exposure to the sun’s UV rays can cause paint to fade and discolor, particularly on south-facing walls. Fading not only dulls the appearance of your home but also indicates that the paint’s protective qualities are diminishing. High-quality, UV-resistant paint is crucial to maintaining vibrant color in Del Rio Texas 's varying climate.
Blistering
Blistering occurs when paint bubbles up from the surface, often due to excessive heat, moisture, or painting over a damp surface. These blisters can burst, leading to peeling and further damage. Proper surface preparation and painting under optimal weather conditions are key to preventing this issue.
Mildew and Mold Growth
In humid areas of Del Rio Texas mildew and mold can grow on exterior surfaces, particularly in shaded areas. This not only affects the appearance of your home but can also lead to structural damage if left untreated. Using mildew-resistant paint and ensuring proper drainage can help prevent these issues.

What causes paint to peel or flake off?

Paint peeling or flaking is a frustrating issue that many homeowners in Del Rio Texas face. Understanding the underlying causes can help you prevent or address these problems effectively. At Painting Vikmanic, we’re here to provide expert solutions to keep your paint job looking pristine.
Moisture Infiltration
One of the leading causes of paint peeling is moisture infiltration. Water entering behind the paint, whether from leaks, condensation, or high humidity, can disrupt the paint’s adhesion, leading to peeling or flaking. This is especially problematic in areas with high moisture exposure, such as bathrooms or exterior walls subjected to rain.
Improper Surface Preparation
Inadequate surface preparation is another critical factor. For paint to adhere properly, the surface must be clean, dry, and free of old paint, dirt, or grease. Without thorough preparation, the new paint may not bond effectively, resulting in peeling or flaking over time.
Low-Quality Paint
Using low-quality paint can also contribute to peeling. Paints that lack durability and flexibility are more prone to failure. Opting for high-quality, durable paint products can significantly reduce the risk of peeling and enhance the longevity of your paint job.
Application Issues
Adverse weather conditions or applying paint too thickly can cause peeling. Extreme temperatures, high humidity, or overly thick layers of paint can hinder proper curing and adhesion, leading to paint failure.
To prevent paint peeling or flaking, ensure proper surface preparation, use high-quality paint, and apply it under optimal conditions.
